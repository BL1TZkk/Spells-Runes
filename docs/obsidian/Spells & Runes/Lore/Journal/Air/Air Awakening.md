I do not know what I was expecting.  
There was no heat, no burning, nothing that would tell me whether or not I should take a deep breath into my chest. It was cold. Not unpleasant. Similar to the first intake of breath when you wake early and are still at high altitude until the sun has reached the bottom of the valley.

I sat quietly for a while. It did not happen. I thought maybe I had messed-up.

And then the wind changed.  
It wasn't coming through an open window. The air in the room moved. Much as if someone who had been holding their breath finally took one. First on the backs of my hands; then on my face; then all over me at the same time. It was not a strong gust. It was more like a presence. As though the air around me had been there the entire time, but had just decided to acknowledge that it existed.

I got up too quickly and had to be seated again. Not from dizziness. From the new realization that I was farther from the ground than I was a few seconds earlier.

I write this down so that tomorrow (or next week) I may be able to recall these events as they occurred. The sensation has begun to fade slightly at the edges of my mind. What remains is that -- something has opened. I don't have another way to describe it. Something that was closed is now open and I don't believe it will close again.
