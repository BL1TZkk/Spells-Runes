Scroll #1

...control before output. The first thing and the only thing until your commanding officer tells you otherwise  
You are here to burn nothing in front of you. Anyone with a torch can do that. You are here because you can do something a torch cannot - you can choose. How much. Where. For how long. These are not instincts yet. They will be. Until then you treat every engagement as if it were controlled exercise. Not release.  
The soldiers who wash out in the first month are not those who lack power. Those who lack power would have already washed out. They are the ones who cannot hold it. Who feel it. And immediately push it outside their body because holding it is uncomfortable. That discomfort is the training. Sit in it.  
In formation, you act independently. You wait for the call. When you get it you execute. And stop when you're told to. Not when you feel like you're done. When you're told.  
A soldier who cannot stop is not a soldier. He's a problem for the enemy to deal with...and we will deal with him ourselves.  
You will spar with non-mage soldiers regularly. This is intentional. You will learn to calibrate -- enough to be effective, not so much that you destroy what's standing next to you. Your unit is not your target. Remember this when things get hot and thinking gets tougher. More on engagements follow in the next section.

Scroll #2

. . . a fire mage who can't feed himself in the field is a liability. The reason this area exists is because we've had liabilities.  
You don't need a fire pit. You don't need flint. You don't need dry wood, good weather, etc. - anything else your unit has been spending their time searching for. That is an advantage and you're going to be using it.  
There is nothing to teach about cooking with your hands. There is little to instruct and lots to permit. Most recruits will figure out how to cook before reaching this point. What prevents them is whether or not they are permitted to.  
They should. Cooking is faster than hunting/fishing/gathering and it leaves no evidence of where you were, giving your unit an option that the enemy cannot anticipate/counter.  
The only issue when it comes to heat is managing it. You are generally not just cooking for yourself. Be aware of the temperature limits for your body and the temperature requirements for the food. Those limits are different.

Other than food (light/warmth/signaling), you are the unit's fire resource in every condition. Weather doesn't prevent you from doing your job; rain won't stop you; wind won't stop you. This isn't ancillary to your position, it will become central to it during prolonged periods in the field. A unit that stays warm at night and can communicate over long distances without equipment has options that units without those capabilities do not.  
Use what you are.
